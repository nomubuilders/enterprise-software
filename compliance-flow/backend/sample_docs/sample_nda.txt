1. PURPOSE

This Non-Disclosure Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into by the above-named parties to protect Confidential Information shared in connection with the joint evaluation of AI-driven regulatory compliance solutions for the European insurance market, specifically targeting compliance with:

  - EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) – Regulation (EU) 2016/679
  - EU Artificial Intelligence Act (AI Act) – Regulation (EU) 2024/1689
  - Dutch Data Protection Act (Uitvoeringswet AVG / UAVG)
  - Spanish Organic Law 3/2018 on Protection of Personal Data (LOPDGDD)
  - EU Digital Operational Resilience Act (DORA) – Regulation (EU) 2022/2554
  - Solvency II Directive – Directive 2009/138/EC

2. DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION
 learning model training data and methodologies

3. OBLIGATIONS OF THE RECEIVING PARTY

The Receiving Party agrees to:
  a) Maintain strict confidentiality of all disclosed information
  b) Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without prior written consent
  c) Limit access to employees and contractors with a legitimate need-to-know
  d) Implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures in accordance with Article 32 GDPR
  e) Conduct a Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) per Article 35 GDPR before processing high-risk personal data
  f) Notify the Disclosing Party within 72 hours of any data breach involving Confidential Information (per Article 33 GDPR)
  g) Appoint a Data Protection Officer (DPO) if required under Article 37 GDPR

4. DATA PROCESSING PROVISIONS (GDPR COMPLIANCE)

4.1 Lawful Basis: Processing of personal data under this agreement is based on legitimate interest (Article 6(1)(f) GDPR) and, where applicable, explicit consent (Article 6(1)(a) GDPR).

4.2 Data Subject Rights: Both parties shall ensure mechanisms are in place to fulfil data subject requests including:
  - Right of access (Article 15)
  - Right to rectification (Article 16)
  - Right to erasure / "Right to be forgotten" (Article 17)
  - Right to data portability (Article 20)
  - Right to object to automated decision-making (Article 22)

4.3 International Transfers: Any transfer of personal data outside the European Economic Area (EEA) shall comply with Chapter V GDPR, utilizing Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) or other approved mechanisms.

4.4 Dutch Specific Requirements (UAVG):
  - BSN (Burgerservicenummer) processing is restricted per Article 46 UAVG
  - Health data processing requires explicit consent per Article 30 UAVG
  - The Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens (Dutch DPA) must be notified of high-risk processing activities

4.5 Spanish Specific Requirements (LOPDGDD):
  - NIE/DNI processing must comply with additional safeguards under Article 9 LOPDGDD
  - Video surveillance data processing follows Article 22 LOPDGDD
  - The Agencia Española de Protección de Datos (AEPD) must be notified in case of breaches

5. AI ACT COMPLIANCE PROVISIONS

5.1 Risk Classification: Any AI system deployed under this agreement that performs automated insurance underwriting or claims assessment shall be classified as "High-Risk" under Annex III of the EU AI Act.

5.2 High-Risk AI Requirements:
  a) Risk management system implementation (Article 9)
  b) Data governance and training data quality assurance (Article 10)
  c) Technical documentation and record-keeping (Article 11-12)
  d) Transparency and provision of information to users (Article 13)
  e) Human oversight mechanisms (Article 14)
  f) Accuracy, robustness, and cybersecurity standards (Article 15)

5.3 Conformity Assessment: Prior to deployment, AI systems must undergo conformity assessment procedures as specified in Article 43.

6. DORA COMPLIANCE (FINANCIAL SECTOR)

6.1 Both parties acknowledge obligations under DORA for ICT risk management:
  a) ICT risk management framework (Article 6)
  b) ICT-related incident management and reporting (Articles 17-23)
  c) Digital operational resilience testing (Articles 24-27)
  d) Third-party ICT service provider oversight (Articles 28-44)

7. TERM AND TERMINATION

7.1 This Agreement shall remain in force for a period of three (3) years from the Effective Date.
7.2 Upon termination, the Receiving Party shall return or destroy all Confidential Information within thirty (30) days.
7.3 Confidentiality obligations survive termination for a period of five (5) years.

8.2 Any disputes shall be submitted to the competent court in Rotterdam, The Netherlands.
8.3 For cross-border enforcement, parties agree to the jurisdiction provisions of Regulation (EU) No 1215/2012 (Brussels I Recast).
